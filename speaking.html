Technical Requirements & Equipment

Prof. Ragel presents from his own laptop with presentation files that often contain embedded video. He will bring a laptop with HDMI/USB-C output and uses modern presentation software.

Required Setup

• HDMI or USB-C connection to projection system
• Audio connection for embedded videos
• Power outlet near presentation area
• Reliable internet access (WiFi or wired)
• Please specify native format and resolution of projection system

Preferred Room Setup

• Wireless lapel or handheld microphone
• Confidence monitor or small table for laptop
• Auditorium seating or round tables (round tables preferred for workshops)
• Audience lights on for better engagement
• No direct light on the screen

Presentation Topics & Formats

Prof. Ragel offers a range of presentations and workshops that can be customized to your event. Most presentations can be extended into half-day or full-day workshops and Master Classes.

Computer Architecture & Modern Computing

Duration: 45-75 minutes • Can be extended to full-day workshop

Titles:
• "The Evolution of Processor Design: From Moore's Law to AI Accelerators"
• "Energy-Efficient Computing for the Modern Era"
• "Hardware-Software Co-Design for AI Applications"

Synopsis: Exploring how computer architecture has adapted to the demands of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and modern computing workloads. Covers processor design evolution, memory system optimization, and energy efficiency challenges.

Interactivity: Optional using audience response systems or show of hands

Embedded Systems & Internet of Things

Duration: 60-90 minutes • Can be extended to full-day workshop

Titles:
• "The Future of IoT and Edge Computing"
• "Real-Time Systems in Modern Applications"
• "Embedded Security: Challenges and Solutions"

Synopsis: Comprehensive overview of embedded systems design, IoT architecture, and edge computing solutions. Discusses real-time operating systems, communication protocols, and security considerations for connected devices.

Interactivity: Hands-on demonstrations available for workshops

Transforming Engineering Education

Duration: 50-90 minutes • Workshop format available (1-6 hours)

Titles:
• "Innovative Teaching Methodologies in Engineering"
• "Project-Based Learning: From Theory to Practice"
• "Educating the Innovators of the 21st Century"
• "Technology Integration in Engineering Programs"

Synopsis: Sharing insights on effective engineering pedagogy, including project-based learning, technology integration, and student engagement strategies. Demonstrates practical approaches that have been successfully implemented in university settings.

Interactivity: Highly interactive with group activities and discussions

Research, Innovation & Technology Transfer

Duration: 60 minutes • Panel discussions available

Titles:
• "Building Technology Ecosystems: Lessons from Academia"
• "Academic-Industry Collaboration for Innovation"
• "From Research to Real-World Impact"
• "Sustainable Technology Development"

Synopsis: Discussing how universities can contribute to regional and national technology development through effective research, innovation ecosystems, and industry partnerships. Shares case studies and practical strategies.

Interactivity: Q&A sessions and panel discussions encouraged
